WHAT THE COLLEGE OF ARMS REQUIRES
================================================================
1. DOCUMENTARY TRACEABILITY
   Every generation linked by primary source.
   No gaps papered over with assumption or narrative.
   Where gaps exist — explicitly marked as UNKNOWN GAP.

2. HERALDIC PEDIGREE VERIFICATION STANDARD
   Patrilineal descent from an armigerous ancestor.
   Arms must be traceable to a specific grant or confirmation.
   The shared motto Te Deum Laudamus across four independent
   Harper/Harpur grants is strong circumstantial evidence of
   common family origin — use carefully, not as sole proof.

3. ARCHIVAL CITATION RIGOR
   Every claim cites: archive + reference + date + document type.
   Secondary sources clearly distinguished from primary.
   No citation that cannot be independently verified.

4. GENERATIONAL CONTINUITY
   Each generation connected to the next by documentary proof.
   Baptism → marriage → will/probate continuity is the ideal.
   Where one link is missing — two flanking links plus
   circumstantial evidence is the minimum acceptable standard.

================================================================

dormant Harpur Baronetcy of Calke Abbey (lapsed 1924).

Key legal facts:
  - Baronetcy created 1626 for Sir Henry Harpur (b.1579)
  - Lapsed 1924 on death of 10th Baronet Sir Vauncey Harpur Crewe
  - Son predeceased 1921 — no direct male heir
  - Never formally declared extinct — status: DORMANT
  - Johnson family (Vermont) claim = female-line only
    Female-line cannot support baronetcy succession under
    the original Letters Patent (male-line descent specified)

The precedent argument:
  The baronetcy's own accepted pedigree contains a documented
  gap of 109 years between confirmable primary sources.
  Our proposed gap is approximately 30 years (c.1507-1538).
  Our evidence density exceeds the baronetcy's own standard.
  This is the legal argument — precedent, not just genealogy.
