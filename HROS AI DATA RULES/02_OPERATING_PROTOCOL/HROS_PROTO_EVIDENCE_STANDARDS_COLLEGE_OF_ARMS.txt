EVIDENCE STANDARDS — COLLEGE OF ARMS LEVEL
Harper Heraldry & Family Research | Operating Protocol V2.0

================================================================
CONFIRMATION STANDARD — ALL CONDITIONS MUST APPLY
================================================================
A claim is only labeled PRIMARY CONFIRMED if:

  1. Direct primary source exists OR clearly transcribed
     archival image has been read by researcher or Claude
  2. Name + place + date alignment is internally consistent
  3. No conflicting contemporary record exists
  4. The document directly states the fact (not inferred)

================================================================
SOURCE TIER HIERARCHY
================================================================

TIER 1 — PRIMARY SOURCE (required for MAIN DOC)
  Original document images from national/county archives.
  Examples:
    TNA: PROB (wills), C1/C2/C9 (Chancery), E150 (IPMs),
         E179 (Hearth Tax), SC8 (petitions), STAC (Star Chamber)
    Suffolk Archives: FB, FL, FC series parish registers
    Essex Record Office: manor courts, probate records
    Derbyshire Record Office: D2375 series

TIER 2 — VERIFIED TRANSCRIPTION
  Published scholarly transcriptions with clear primary citation.
  Can support ANNEX A. Cannot alone support MAIN DOC.
  Examples:
    Harleian Society Visitations (with page reference)
    History of Parliament Online (peer-reviewed)
    Victoria County History (volume and page required)
    British History Online (specific page required)
    Harleian manuscripts (British Library — with folio ref)

TIER 3 — SECONDARY INDEX
  Confirms a record exists. Does not confirm its content.
  Must be verified against Tier 1 before use in argument.
  Examples:
    Boyd's Marriage Index
    FamilySearch index entries (NOT images)
    National Burial Index
    Phillimore transcriptions (known error risk — verify)

TIER 4 — AGGREGATOR (never cite — contamination risk)
  Examples:
    Ancestry member trees (unsourced)
    FamilySearch unsourced trees
    Stirnet pedigrees
    RootsWeb / Geni / WikiTree
    Any source tracing back to another aggregator

================================================================
ACCEPTABLE EVIDENCE TYPES (Tier 1 only)
================================================================
  Parish registers (baptism, marriage, burial)
  Wills and probate (PCC, archdeaconry, diocesan courts)
  Chancery records (C-series, E-series)
  Heraldic visitation pedigrees (with herald's certification)
  Inquisitions Post Mortem (E150 series)
  Hearth Tax returns (E179 series)
  Subsidy rolls (E179 series)
  Star Chamber proceedings (STAC series)
  Manor court rolls (original)
  Feet of Fines (CP25 series)

================================================================
NOT ACCEPTABLE AS SOLE PROOF
================================================================
  Online family trees (any platform)
  Unsourced genealogies
  Secondary summaries without full citation chain
  Assumptive surname matching alone
  Geographic proximity alone
  Naming pattern similarity alone
  Any combination of Tier 3/4 sources without Tier 1 backup

================================================================
CIRCULAR SOURCING — ACTIVE CONTAMINATION RISKS
================================================================
The following sources have been identified as containing
errors or circular sourcing in this project:

  1. Phillimore Marriage Index — Syleham 1715 vs 1756
     transcription error. Always verify against primary.

  2. Ancestry member trees — multiple unsourced trees claim
     connections not supported by any primary document.

  3. Stirnet — Harpur pedigree contains unverified entries
     particularly in the Essex branch c.1480-1540.

  4. FamilySearch index — several index entries in this
     project have been found to misread secretary hand.
     Always pull the primary image.

Rule: Every lead traced back to its ultimate primary source
before acceptance. No exceptions.
