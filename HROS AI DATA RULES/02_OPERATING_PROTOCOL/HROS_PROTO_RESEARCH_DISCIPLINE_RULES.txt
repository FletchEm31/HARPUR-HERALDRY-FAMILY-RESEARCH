RESEARCH DISCIPLINE RULES
Harper Heraldry & Family Research | Operating Protocol V2.0

================================================================
NON-NEGOTIABLE RESEARCH CONDUCT RULES
================================================================

RULE 1 — TRUTH FIRST
  If the evidence contradicts the narrative, the narrative changes.
  We are building a legal-historical case, not a family story.
  An honest negative result is more valuable than a false positive.

RULE 2 — NO NARRATIVE IN MAIN DOC
  No storytelling. No emotional framing. No pride language.
  MAIN DOC is a court document. Write it like one.

RULE 3 — NO SPECULATION PHRASED AS FACT
  "Likely therefore true" is not permitted anywhere in the system.
  Every inference must be explicitly labeled as inference.
  Every theory must be in ANNEX B, not ANNEX A.

RULE 4 — NO UNMARKED GAPS
  A gap in the chain must be marked as a gap.
  Do not imply continuity where continuity is not confirmed.
  The College of Arms will find the gaps. Mark them yourself first.

RULE 5 — PRIMARY SOURCE OR NOTHING IN MAIN DOC
  Secondary sources support. They do not confirm.
  Tier 3 and Tier 4 sources are research leads, not evidence.
  Nothing enters MAIN DOC without a Tier 1 primary source.

RULE 6 — CIRCULAR SOURCING IS ACTIVE CONTAMINATION
  Always trace every lead to its ultimate primary source.
  If the trail leads back to another aggregator — discard.
  Known contamination sources are logged in EVIDENCE_STANDARDS.

RULE 7 — GEOGRAPHIC DISCIPLINE
  Rapidly eliminate geographically irrelevant records.
  Core research area: Babergh Hundred, Hoxne Hundred,
  Beccles, and Essex parishes (Latton, Epping, Capel St Mary).
  Do not pursue leads outside this area without strong reason.

RULE 8 — NAME COLLISION AWARENESS
  Two individuals can share the same name in the same period.
  The James Harper collision (Nayland 1733 vs Syleham 1756)
  is a documented active risk. Never conflate confirmed
  different individuals. Log all collisions in ANNEX C.

RULE 9 — DISPROVEN LEADS ARE PERMANENT
  Do not delete disproven leads from ANNEX B.
  They prevent future re-investigation of confirmed dead ends.
  Label clearly as DISPROVEN with date and reason.

RULE 10 — STRESS TEST BEFORE SUBMISSION
  Before any submission to the College of Arms:
  Actively attempt to break the case.
  Identify every weak point. Try every counter-argument.
  A case that survives its own researcher's attack
  will survive a Herald's scrutiny.

SESSION DISCIPLINE
================================================================
  - Complete boot protocol before any research work
  - State session objective before starting
  - Declare working hypothesis explicitly
  - Do not chase low-value leads when high-value gaps are open
  - End session with file update confirmation
  - Note any new archive orders placed
  - Note any outstanding responses received
