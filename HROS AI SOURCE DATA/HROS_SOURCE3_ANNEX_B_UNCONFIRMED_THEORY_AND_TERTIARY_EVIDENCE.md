3. THE NARRATIVE — How John Harper Gets to Boxford
Richard Harper of Epping dies 1492/3. His elder son Richard III inherits Latton Hall and marries Constance Chamberlayne. Richard III dies 10 April 1509. His son George becomes ward — purchased by stepfather Alexander Culpeper. Essex main line will expire with George (dies 1558, no male heirs).

Richard of Epping's younger son John inherits Essex lands at Stanford and Stapulford on 10 April 1509 — the exact date Richard III dies, activating the contingency in PROB 11/9/246. John is approximately 29-39 years old. He has Essex lands but no great estate. He looks to Suffolk.

Why Suffolk specifically: Constance Chamberlayne — his sister-in-law, now widow of his brother — has family estates at Capel St Mary (8.5 miles from Boxford). Her new husband Alexander Culpeper has church connections at Bures St Mary (4 miles from Boxford, per his will PROB 11/30). The wider Harberd gentry network is already established in Suffolk — William Harbard of Sutton (PROB 11/132/106) confirms gentry Harberds 25 miles from Boxford by 1571.

John moves to Suffolk c.1507-1530. He appears at Boxford as John Harbartt — the surname phonetically drifting through Suffolk dialect. He is not hiding — he simply arrived in a county where clerks wrote names as they heard them spoken. His son John Harbarde marries Rose Smythe at Boxford in 1563. His grandson Josephe Harberd marries Marye Pulley at Boxford in 1598. The chain runs unbroken to Hayden F. Harper, California, 1992.
