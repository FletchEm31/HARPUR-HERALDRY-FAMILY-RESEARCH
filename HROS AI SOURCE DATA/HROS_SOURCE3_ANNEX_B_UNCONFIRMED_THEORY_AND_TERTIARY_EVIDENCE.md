3. THE NARRATIVE — How John Harper Gets to Boxford
================================================================

Richard Harper of Epping dies 1492/3. His elder son Richard III
inherits Latton Hall and marries Constance Chamberlayne. Richard
III dies 10 April 1509. His son George becomes ward — purchased
by stepfather Alexander Culpeper. Essex main line expires with
George (dies 1558, no male heirs).

Richard of Epping's younger son John inherits Essex lands at
Stanford and Stapulford on 10 April 1509 — the exact date Richard
III dies, activating the contingency in PROB 11/9/246. John is
approximately 29-39 years old.

Why Suffolk specifically: Constance Chamberlayne — his
sister-in-law, now widow of his brother — has family estates at
Capel St Mary (8.5 miles from Boxford). Her new husband Alexander
Culpeper has church connections at Bures St Mary (4 miles from
Boxford, per his will PROB 11/30). The wider Harberd gentry
network is already established in Suffolk — William Harbard of
Sutton (PROB 11/132/106) confirms gentry Harberds 25 miles from
Boxford by 1571.

John moves to Suffolk c.1507-1530. He appears at Boxford as
John Harbartt — the surname phonetically drifting through Suffolk
dialect. His son John Harbarde marries Rose Smythe at Boxford
1563. His grandson Josephe Harberd marries Marye Pulley at
Boxford 1598. The chain runs unbroken to Hayden F. Harper,
California, 1992.

9. CHAMBERLAYNE GEOGRAPHIC MECHANISM
================================================================

Status: WORKING THEORY | INFERENCE ONLY
Sources: PROB 11/30 (Alexander Culpeper will 1541) +
         PROB 11/29/217 (Constance Colepepur will 1542)

Sir Alexander Culpeper named five church bequests — one to
St Mary Bures, Suffolk. Bures is 4 miles from Boxford. His wife
Dame Constance (née Chamberlayne) had family estates at Capel
St Mary — 8.5 miles from Boxford.

Two independent primary sources confirm the Chamberlayne family's
Suffolk geographic footprint — placing known connections of John
Harper Esq's extended family within walking distance of Boxford.

CERTAINTY: INFERENCE ONLY — no primary source directly links
John Harper Esq to this geography.
