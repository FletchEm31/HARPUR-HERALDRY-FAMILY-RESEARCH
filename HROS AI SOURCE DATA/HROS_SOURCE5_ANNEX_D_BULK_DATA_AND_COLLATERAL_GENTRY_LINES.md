1. Sir Charles Harberd kt — HM Surveyor General under Charles II
Sir Charles Harberd kt was HM Surveyor General under King Charles II — a significant Crown appointment. He was resident at St Martin in the Fields, Middlesex, and appears in four TNA Chancery and probate cases spanning 1625–1679. He is knighted and connected to Chancery litigation involving major aristocratic figures (Earl of Southampton, Earl of Pembroke, Lord Pawlett).
Sir Charles is almost certainly collateral to the Harberd family network that includes Josephe Harberd of Boxford and Francis Harberd of Long Melford/Ufford/Bredfield. He is NOT a confirmed direct ancestor. His probate record (PROB 18/11/62, 1679) is the highest priority TNA document — it will name his heirs and potentially map the wider Harberd family structure of the mid-17th century.
SIGNIFICANCE: A confirmed connection to Sir Charles Harberd kt would provide independent evidence of armigerous gentry standing for the wider Harberd family at exactly the period of your direct ancestor Josephe Harberd of Boxford. This strengthens the overall petition even if Sir Charles is collateral only.

2. Francis Harberd — Land in Long Melford, Ufford \& Bredfield
Francis Harberd held 200 acres across Long Melford (8 miles from Boxford), Ufford and Bredfield (near Hollesley) per TNA C 2/Eliz/M7/4 (Chancery, Mawe v Francis Harberd, 1558–1603). This places a Harberd landowner with substantial Suffolk holdings at exactly the geographic cluster of the wider Harberd network — Boxford, Hollesley, and the Woodbridge area — in the same generation as Josephe Harberd of Boxford (b. c.1560s).
Francis Harberd is almost certainly a brother or cousin of Josephe Harberd of Boxford. Together with John Harberd of Hollesley (Suffolk Visitation 1612), these three Harberds — Josephe, John, Francis — form what is almost certainly a single extended Suffolk gentry family active c.1560–1620.
