1. CRITICAL CORRECTION — John Harper Generational Position
CORRECTED April 2026: John Harper Esq is the YOUNGER SON of Sir Richard Harpur of Epping (PROB 11/9/246) — not the younger brother of Sir George Harper MP. George is the son of Richard III (grandson of Richard of Epping). John is Richard of Epping's second son — making John the UNCLE of George, one generation above him.

This correction strengthens the timeline: John born c.1470-1480, inherits Essex lands c.1507 on Richard III's death, moves to Suffolk c.1507-1530, appears at Boxford as John Harbartt fl.c.1510-1540. Perfectly consistent with a man in his 30s-40s relocating after inheriting.

2. NEW PRIMARY SOURCE DOCUMENTS — April 2026
PROB 11/59/108 — Will of Richard Harpur alias Harper, Justice of Common Pleas, Swarkeston, Derbyshire. 28 January 1577.
Free download from TNA Discovery. Three pages. Primary image confirmed April 2026.

Opens: "In the name of the Eternall God the ffather... I Richard Harpur als Harper of Swarkeston out of her majesties Justices of the common pleas at Westminster... the 7th of July 1576..."

Person | Relationship | Notes
Jane Harpur | Wife and executrix | Named throughout; life interest in all goods and chattels
John Harpur | Eldest son | Inherits Swarkeston; receives law books and coffers from study
Richard Harpur | Second son | Inherits manor of Starkastle, Yorkshire; lands in Milton, Derbyshire
Henry Harpur | Third son | Named in bequests
Dorothie | Wife (also named) | Household goods during widowhood



CRITICAL SIGNIFICANCE: This document uses HARPUR ALIAS HARPER in a single primary source. The Justice of the Common Pleas himself used both spellings interchangeably. This is definitive primary source evidence that Harpur and Harper are the same name — destroying any argument that they represent different families. The College of Arms cannot dispute a spelling variant that the family itself used simultaneously in a legal document.

John Harpur (eldest son) is Sir John Harpur of Swarkeston — father of Sir Henry Harpur, 1st Baronet of Calke Abbey (1579-1638). This document confirms the direct baronetcy line by primary source.

CRITICAL CONFIRMATION: George Harpur is named as her son from the Harper marriage — separately and distinctly from the Culpeper children. This is primary source confirmation that Constance Chamberlayne's first husband was a Harper, and their son was George Harpur (Sir George Harper MP, 1503-1558, History of Parliament confirmed).

NOTE ON JOHN: John Harper Esq (younger son of Richard of Epping) does NOT appear in this will — because he is Constance's BROTHER-IN-LAW, not her son. He is one generation above George. He would have been settled long before this will was made in 1541. His absence is fully consistent with the corrected generational structure.

PROB 11/30 (PCC 30 Alenger) — Will of Sir Alexander Culpeper of Bedgebury. Proved 21 June 1541.
Free download from TNA Discovery. Primary image available.

CRITICAL GEOGRAPHIC FINDING: Alexander's will contains a specific bequest of a vestment to "the churche of Sainte Marye Bewers in the County of Suffolke" — St Mary's Bures, Suffolk. Bures is 4 miles from Boxford.

The Culpeper/Chamberlayne family had a direct Suffolk church connection at the doorstep of our confirmed Harberd chain. Constance's brother-in-law John Harper Esq settling near Boxford would be settling near his family's known Suffolk connections. Geographic convergence: Capel St Mary (Chamberlayne estates, 8.5 miles from Boxford) + Bures St Mary (Culpeper bequest, 4 miles from Boxford) + Boxford (confirmed Harberd chain).
