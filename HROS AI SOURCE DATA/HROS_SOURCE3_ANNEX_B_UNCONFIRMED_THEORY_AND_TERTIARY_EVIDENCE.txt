3. THE NARRATIVE — How John Harper Gets to Boxford
================================================================
Richard Harper of Epping dies 1492/3. His elder son Richard III inherits Latton Hall and
marries Constance Chamberlayne. Richard III dies 10 April 1509. His son George becomes ward
— purchased by stepfather Alexander Culpeper. Essex main line will expire with George (dies
1558, no male heirs).

Richard of Epping's younger son John inherits Essex lands at Stanford and Stapulford on 10
April 1509 — the exact date Richard III dies, activating the contingency in PROB 11/9/246.
John is approximately 29-39 years old. He has Essex lands but no great estate. He looks to
Suffolk.

Why Suffolk specifically: Constance Chamberlayne — his sister-in-law, now widow of his
brother — has family estates at Capel St Mary (8.5 miles from Boxford). Her new husband
Alexander Culpeper has church connections at Bures St Mary (4 miles from Boxford, per his
will PROB 11/30). The wider Harberd gentry network is already established in Suffolk.

NEW 30 APR 2026: Multiple Chancery cases now place a John Harper actively operating as a
moneylender and property holder in Suffolk in exactly this window. His executors are named
Nicholas and Jeffrey — almost certainly his sons. Jeffrey appears as a baptism name one
generation later inside the Boxford Harbertt family. His son Thomas Harper is confirmed
pursuing John's Suffolk estate in Chancery in 1553-1555. His death is placed approximately
1540-1550 based on Thomas's active pursuit of the estate by 1553.
