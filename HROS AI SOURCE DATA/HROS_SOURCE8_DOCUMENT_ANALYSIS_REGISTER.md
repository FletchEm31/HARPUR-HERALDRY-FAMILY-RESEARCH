DOCUMENT 1
WP-TNA-001
PROB 11/9/246 — Will of Richard Harpur of Epping, Essex
Proved: January 1492/3
Court: Prerogative Court of Canterbury
Primary image read: April 17 2026
================================

VERBATIM EXTRACTS
-----------------

Opening (Latin):
  "In dei nomine Amen... Ricardus Harper de Epping in Com
  Essex... sane mentis et bone memorie existens... condo
  presens testamentum meum p voluntatem"

  [In the name of God Amen... Richard Harper of Epping in the
  County of Essex... being of sound mind and good memory...
  I make this present testament by my will]

Burial bequest (Latin):
  "lego fabrice Ecclie poch Bte Marie de Latton"

  [I bequeath to the fabric of the parish church of Blessed
  Mary of Latton]

Bequest to All Saints Epping (Latin):
  "lego fabrice Ecclie Omni Sancti de Epping"

  [I bequeath to the fabric of the church of All Saints
  of Epping]

Bequest to Rushall — CRITICAL (Latin):
  "lego fabrice Ecclie Sancti Petri de Rushall in Com Stafford"

  [I bequeath to the fabric of the church of Saint Peter
  of Rushall in the County of Stafford]

Sons named (Latin):
  "Johannes Harper filio meo"
  [John Harper my son]

  "Ricardi Harper filio meo Tenendisei at heredis assignat"
  [Richard Harper my son, to hold to him and his heirs
  assigned]

Widow named (Latin):
  "Domine Elizabethi vxori mie"
  [Dame Elizabeth my wife]

Grandson reference (Latin):
  "nepoti meo filio filii"
  [my grandson, son of my son]

Probate section (Latin):
  "Probatū fuit hoc testamentum... apud Cantuariam...
  die mensis Januarij Anno Dñi Millesimo [1492/3]...
  Et commissa fuit Administratio... Domine Elizabethe Relicte"

  [This will was proved... at Canterbury... the day of the
  month of January in the year of our Lord 1492/3...
  And administration was committed... to Dame Elizabeth,
  relict (widow)]


SIGNIFICANCE
------------

This is the foundational document of the entire research.

1. JOHN HARPER NAMED AS SON.
"Johannes Harper filio meo" — John Harper is explicitly named
as a son of Richard Harpur of Epping. This is the John Harper
who is our proposed ancestor — the younger son who inherits
Essex lands on his brother Richard III's death in 1509 and,
on our hypothesis, subsequently migrates to Suffolk. His
existence and parentage are confirmed at primary source level
in a Prerogative Court of Canterbury will — the highest
possible evidentiary standard for a pre-parish register
document.

2. THE RUSHALL CHURCH BEQUEST.
Richard Harpur of Epping — a man who lived his entire adult
life in Essex — left a specific financial bequest to the
Church of Saint Peter at Rushall, Staffordshire,
approximately 150 miles from his home. A man does not leave
money to a church 150 miles distant without a direct family
connection. Rushall is the documented ancestral seat of the
Harpur family from whom the Baronets of Calke Abbey descend.
This bequest is primary source evidence that the Essex Harper
line and the Derbyshire Harpur Baronet line share a common
Rushall origin. The Kent Visitation of 1574 independently
confirms this through heraldic record. Two independent primary
sources pointing to the same origin. This is not coincidence.

3. ELDER AND YOUNGER SON STRUCTURE CONFIRMED.
Richard is named as the elder son (inheriting Latton Hall)
and John as the younger son (with contingency rights to the
Essex lands at Stanford and Stapulford). When Richard III dies
in 1509 with no male heirs, John's contingency rights activate.
John is a man in his late twenties or early thirties, with
inherited land but no great house, and a mother whose family
held estates at Capel St Mary, Suffolk — eight and a half miles
from Boxford. The conditions for migration to Suffolk are
established by this will.

4. GRANDSON REFERENCE.
"Nepoti meo filio filii" — a grandson described as son of his
son — suggests at least one of Richard's sons already had
children by 1492. If John Harper was born c.1470-1480, he
could have had a child by 1492 who would be the right age to
appear at Boxford as John Harbertt c.1510-1540. This is not
confirmation — it is a consistent data point.

5. DAME ELIZABETH AS WIDOW.
Dame Elizabeth — confirmed elsewhere as daughter of Sir Piers
Arderne — is named as executrix. Her family's Essex gentry
connections provide relevant social context for the status
of the Harper family in this period.

DOCUMENT 2
WP-TNA-002
PROB 11/59/108 — Will of Richard Harpur alias Harper
Justice of Common Pleas, Swarkeston, Derbyshire
Dated: July 1576 | Proved: 7 February 1576/7
Court: Prerogative Court of Canterbury
Primary image read: April 17 2026
================================

VERBATIM EXTRACTS
-----------------

Opening — CRITICAL SPELLING PROOF:
  "I Richard Harpur als Harper of Swarkfton out of his
  majesties Justices of the common plees at Westminster"

  [I Richard Harpur alias Harper of Swarkeston, one of his
  majesty's Justices of the Common Pleas at Westminster]

Religious preamble:
  "In the name of the Eternall God the ffather"

Burial instructions:
  "my body to be buried without pompt or choft by the promose
  of my Executor to and out of his new Chappell in Swarkfton
  Church"

  [my body to be buried without pomp or cost... in his new
  Chapel in Swarkston Church]

Eldest son named:
  "thereofunto my Eldest Sonne John"
  [thereof unto my Eldest Son John]

Second son — Derbyshire and Yorkshire property:
  "Starklafte in the Countie of yorke and my ffflame in
  Milten in the Countie of Diby"

  [Starkcastle in the County of York and my lands in Milton
  in the County of Derby]

Wife's life interest:
  "havinge That my hartely beloved wife have the yse of all
  my goodes and Chattells during her widowhood"

  [having that my heartily beloved wife have the use of all
  my goods and chattels during her widowhood]

Step son — separate individual, noted:
  "And unto John my ftep fonne"
  [And unto John my step son]

Executors confirmed:
  "j confitme Executor mie faid wife Jane Harpur and Dyrharde
  harper my faid fonne"

  [I confirm as Executors my said wife Jane Harpur and
  Richard Harper my said son]

Codicil — signed personally:
  "Addcobyme the withing named Dyrhards Harpur ffatiger this
  yere Sfaife Daye of Sainte Lulie the Evangeliste 1576"

  [Added by me the within named Richard Harpur... the feast
  day of Saint Luke the Evangelist 1576]

Probate section (Latin):
  "Probatum fuit huiusd Testamentum apud London... Die mienst
  ffebruarij A ñio Dñi millo Quingentesimo Sexto"

  [This will was proved at London... the day of the month of
  February in the year of our Lord 1576/7]


SIGNIFICANCE
------------

1. HARPUR ALIAS HARPER IN A SINGLE LEGAL DOCUMENT.
The Justice of the Common Pleas — one of the most senior
judges in England — described himself in his own will as
"Richard Harpur alias Harper." This is not a clerk's
transcription variant or a copyist's error. This is the man
himself, in his own legal instrument of record, using both
spellings simultaneously as interchangeable descriptions of
a single person. The College of Arms cannot argue that Harpur
and Harper represent different families when a primary source
of this authority demonstrates both names as one. This single
line of text is among the most valuable documents in the
entire research file.

2. CONFIRMS THE DIRECT BARONETCY LINE.
John — the eldest son who inherits Swarkeston — is Sir John
Harpur of Swarkeston, confirmed father of Sir Henry Harpur,
1st Baronet of Calke Abbey (created 1626). This document
places us one generation above the 1st Baronet with primary
source confirmation. The baronetcy succession is anchored
here.

3. STEP SON JOHN — CRITICAL DISTINCTION.
The will names "John my step son" separately and distinctly
from "John my eldest son." These are two different
individuals. The step son has no blood connection to the
Harpur family and no relevance to the baronetcy line or our
Suffolk chain. This distinction must be maintained in all
future analysis. Any confusion of the two Johns would
introduce a false individual into the chain.

4. SWARKSTON MANOR CONTINUITY.
The estate at Swarkston named in this 1576 will is the same
estate charged with Lady Frances Harpur's annuity in the
6th Baronet's will of 1789 (WP-TNA-017). The Harpur family
held continuous possession of Swarkston from the 16th century
through to the late 18th century — a 213-year documented chain
of single family ownership confirmed across two primary
source wills.

DOCUMENT 3
WP-TNA-017
PROB 11/1175/248 — Will and Codicil of Sir Henry Harpur
6th Baronet of Calke Abbey, Derbyshire
Dated: 4 February 1789 | Proved: 14 February 1789
Court: Prerogative Court of Canterbury
Primary image read: April 17 2026
================================

VERBATIM EXTRACTS
-----------------

Opening identification:
  "This is the last Will and Testament of me Sir Henry Harpur
  of Calke in the County of Derby Baronet"

Wife's annuity:
  "I give and devise and bequeath to my Wife Lady Frances
  Harpur an Annuity or rent charge of Three hundred pounds
  a year for her life clear of all taxes and deductions
  whatsoever to be added to the Twelve hundred pounds a year
  settled upon her by our Marriage Settlement"

Son and heir:
  "until my only Son Sir Henry Harpur attains the age of
  one and twenty years"

Codicil — legatees:
  "Mrs Rachel Tracy" — £200 guineas legacy
  "William Holmes" — wages and one year's additional wages

Executors:
  "Martha Bummings [Cummings]" — administration granted
  "Elizabetha Bummings" — daughter, co-executor, power
  reserved

Proved before:
  "William Battine Doctor of Laws Surrogate of the Right
  Worshipfull Sir William Wynne Knight Doctor of Laws...
  Commissary of the Prerogative Court of Canterbury"

Probate grant:
  "constituted by the Oath of Martha Bummings one of the
  Executors named in the said will to whom Administration
  was granted"


SIGNIFICANCE
------------

This document is collateral to the direct Suffolk chain.
Its value to the research is contextual and confirmatory.

1. 6TH BARONET SUCCESSION CONFIRMED.
The will proves Sir Henry Harpur (1739-1789) as the 6th
Baronet, with his son Sir Henry Harpur (later 7th Baronet,
subsequently Harpur Crewe) as his heir. This fills a
documented gap in the baronetcy succession table and
confirms the line of descent through the 18th century.

2. SWARKSTON MANOR CONFIRMED IN CONTINUOUS HARPUR POSSESSION.
The wife's annuity is charged on the Manor of Swarkston —
the same manor that appears in PROB 11/59/108 (Richard
Harpur alias Harper's will, 1576). From 1576 to 1789 is
213 years. Two primary source wills, separated by over two
centuries, both charging obligations on the same estate held
by the same family. This is extraordinary documentary
continuity and confirms the unbroken Harpur possession of
Swarkston across the entire baronetcy period.

3. SCALE AND STATUS FOR DR. FOX.
The 6th Baronet's estate generates sufficient income to
provide his widow with an annuity of £1,200 per year in
1789 — a substantial sum. The Calke Abbey baronetcy was not
a paper title. It represented a significant landed estate,
active gentry status, and continuous family presence in
Derbyshire across three centuries. This context is important
for understanding the nature of the dormant title being
researched.

DOCUMENT 4
WP-SFK-005
IC/500/2/49/100 — Will of Christopher Harper
Bury St Edmunds, Suffolk
Dated: 9 August 1614 | Proved: 22 October 1614
Court: Archdeaconry of Sudbury
Primary image read: April 17 2026
================================

VERBATIM EXTRACTS
-----------------

Opening:
  "In the name of god Amen. The Nyntee daie of August in
  the yere of our Lord god one Thousand five Hundred and
  ffoureteend [1614] in the yere of the Reigne of our
  Sovereign Lord James by the grace of god of England
  Fraunce and Ireland Kinge defender of the ffaithe and
  of Scotland the eight and fortieth yere. I Christophor
  Harper of Burie Saint Edm in the Countie of Suff"

  [In the name of God Amen. The Ninth day of August in the
  year of our Lord God one Thousand six Hundred and
  Fourteen, in the year of the reign of our Sovereign Lord
  James... I Christopher Harper of Bury Saint Edmunds in
  the County of Suffolk]

Sons named with legacies:
  "Item I give and bequeath unto Christophor Harper my
  eldest Sonne the some of Thirtie pounds"

  "Item I give and bequeath unto Frauncis Harper my Sonne
  the some of Thirtie pounds"

Brothers named — CRITICAL:
  "equally divided amongst Ales Harper my lovinge wife and
  so mainie of the Children of Thomas Harper William Harper
  and Andrew Harp my Brothers as then Shalle lyveinge"

  [equally divided amongst Alice Harper my loving wife and
  so many of the children of Thomas Harper William Harper
  and Andrew Harper my Brothers as shall then be living]

Wife as executrix:
  "said Alice Harper my wife and Executor"

Testator signed with mark:
  "Christophor Harpers marke"

Additional executor:
  "Nippeas Hearte of Burie aforesaid Maltster"

Witnesses:
  "Jopon Bishope" and "Jopu Langdole"

Probate (Latin):
  "Probatū fuit huidi Testamentū apud Bury Sti Edi...
  xxij die mensis Octobris Anno dñi 1614"

  [This will was proved at Bury St Edmunds... the 22nd
  day of the month of October in the year of our Lord 1614]

  Before surrogate Johannes Howell, notary Barnabas Borge,
  Archdeaconry of Sudbury, town of Bury St Edmunds.


SIGNIFICANCE
------------

1. FOUR HARPER BROTHERS CONFIRMED AT BURY ST EDMUNDS 1614.
Christopher, Thomas, William, and Andrew Harper are named
as brothers in a primary source will, proved at Bury
St Edmunds in October 1614. This is a Harper family cluster
of four brothers confirmed in exactly the right place at
exactly the right time — Bury St Edmunds, one generation
before our confirmed chain entry point.

2. ONE GENERATION BEFORE JOHN HARBAR 1623.
The confirmed chain entry at Bury St Edmunds is John Harbar
x Mary Bathorne, married 26 November 1623 at the same
parish. Christopher Harper's will is proved 22 October 1614
— nine years earlier, same location. The Harper brothers of
1614 are one generation above John Harbar's 1623 marriage.
If John Harbar is the son of Thomas, William, or Andrew
Harper — one of Christopher's named brothers — the
geographic and temporal alignment is exact. This connection
remains unconfirmed but the circumstantial case is strong.

3. CHRISTOPHER SIGNED WITH A MARK.
Christopher Harper could not write his own name — he signed
with a mark. This places the family in the artisan or
yeoman class rather than the gentry at this point, which
is consistent with the gradual social descent expected of
a junior branch over four to five generations from the
Essex gentry line.

4. COLLEGE OF ARMS STANDARD EVIDENCE.
This is a primary source document, proved at the
Archdeaconry of Sudbury with named witnesses and a named
surrogate. It meets the evidentiary standard required for
the College of Arms submission. The connection to John
Harbar requires one further document to confirm — a will
or baptism record naming John Harbar's father as one of
these brothers.

WP-SFK-006  [ID TO BE FORMALLY ASSIGNED]
IC/500/5/1/4/4f — Administration of Hugo Harbarde
Boxford, Suffolk
Died: 9 January 1570/71
Administration granted: November/December 1570
Court: Archdeaconry of Sudbury
Primary image read: April 17 2026
================================

VERBATIM EXTRACTS
-----------------

Register header:
  "Novembris Anno 1570"
  [November in the year 1570]

Administration entry (Latin):
  "Hugo Harbarde numo dñi spirt de Boxforde abintestat
  Decessit Nono die mensis Januarij Anno dñi"

  [Hugo Harbarde, of Boxford, died intestate on the ninth
  day of the month of January in the year of our lord]

Administration granted to widow (Latin):
  "Emanuit Administracio bonorum Dñi defuncti Doreti Harpeth
  eius relicte"

  [Administration of the goods of the said deceased was
  granted to Doreti Harpeth [Dorothy Harper] his
  relict/widow]

Guarantor named (Latin):
  "in persona Willelmi"
  [in the person of William]

Margin annotation:
  "Boxford"


SIGNIFICANCE
------------

1. HARBARDE AND HARPER IN THE SAME HOUSEHOLD AT BOXFORD.
Hugo Harbarde of Boxford died intestate in January 1570/71.
His widow — granted administration of his estate — is named
"Doreti Harpeth" — Dorothy Harper by maiden name. This is
primary source confirmation that a member of the Harbarde
family of Boxford married a woman born Harper. The two
surname variants — Harbarde and Harper — existed in the
same household simultaneously. A husband named Harbarde
and a wife born Harper, at Boxford, in 1570.

2. DIRECT EVIDENCE FOR THE SINGLE FAMILY HYPOTHESIS.
The core argument of this research is that Harbarde,
Harberd, Harbar, Harbor, and Harper are phonetic variants
of the same surname — representing a single family that
migrated from Essex to Suffolk and whose name was rendered
differently by different clerks across different decades.
This administration record provides documentary evidence
of the two variants being used for members of the same
immediate family in the same parish at the same time. This
is not a theoretical argument. This is a primary source
document.

3. TIMING AND PROXIMITY TO THE CONFIRMED CHAIN.
Hugo Harbarde died 1570/71. His brother John Harbarde
married Rose Smythe at Boxford in 1563 — seven years
earlier, the same parish. Both brothers are active at
Boxford in the same decade. John Harbarde is our confirmed
chain ancestor (Generation 5 in the primary chain).
Hugo is his brother — confirmed collateral. Dorothy Harper,
Hugo's widow, bears the surname that our hypothesis
proposes is the origin form of all Harberd variants. Her
presence in the Boxford Harbarde family in 1570 makes the
Harper/Harberde connection a documented fact at parish
level, not a theoretical inference.

4. WILLIAM THE GUARANTOR.
The guarantor named in the administration bond is
"Willelmus" — William. He may be a brother or kinsman of
Hugo. The name William appears repeatedly across the
Harper/Harberd network in this period and geography.
Not yet confirmed but worth noting for future research.
